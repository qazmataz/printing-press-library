# Novel-Features Brainstorm (subagent output, 2026-06-10)

## Customer model

**Mara, UA manager at a mid-size match-3 studio** (brief Users: "UA managers"; Top Workflows 1 and 6)
- **Today (without this CLI):** Monday mornings she has the AppMagic web app open with ~10 competitor profile tabs (Royal Match, Candy Crush Saga, Gardenscapes class of apps), copies downloads/revenue/retention numbers into a spreadsheet one app at a time, and flips to Ad Intelligence to eyeball new creatives. She cannot answer "what changed in my set since last Monday" without keeping her own export history.
- **Weekly ritual:** A Monday competitor pull: downloads, revenue, DAU, retention for a fixed set of united apps in US and WW, plus a scan of competitor live-ops events and creatives, assembled into a deck for the UA standup.
- **Frustration:** Re-pulling the same per-app charts one by one every week and hand-diffing against last week's spreadsheet. The API has a batchable `requests[]` envelope but nothing remembers her set.

**Deniz, market analyst at a game publisher** (brief Users: "game studio analysts"; Top Workflows 2 and 3)
- **Today (without this CLI):** Opens top free/grossing charts for 3 countries x 2 stores, scrolls looking for unfamiliar icons, and keeps last week's CSV export to compare in Excel. Soft-launch scouting means manually scanning the soft-launches chart in PH/CA/AU and trying to remember what was there last time.
- **Weekly ritual:** Monday movers report: who entered the top 50 grossing, who dropped, which publishers are pushing new titles, what new soft launches appeared in test markets.
- **Frustration:** The charts API is stateless snapshots. "Who ENTERED the top 50 since last week" is not a question any single endpoint answers; it requires keeping history the vendor does not keep for you.

**Priya, agency-side ASO specialist** (brief Users: "ASO specialists"; Top Workflow 5; Community Signal complaint 3)
- **Today (without this CLI):** Per client app, pulls ASO keyword positions from the web UI or one-off API calls, pastes into a rank tracker, and computes week-over-week deltas by hand. She found out her agency's contract excludes ASA only when a call returned a bare 403.
- **Weekly ritual:** Weekly keyword report per client: which keywords gained rank, which were lost, which new keywords the app started ranking for, per country and store.
- **Frustration:** Rank deltas are hand-computed, and entitlement opacity (G2/OMR complaint: tier gating is not transparent up front) means she cannot tell which endpoint groups her login can even use.

**Tomas, market researcher / investor analyst** (brief Users: "market researchers, investors/analysts"; Top Workflows 3, 4, 7; Community Signal complaints 1 and 2)
- **Today (without this CLI):** Builds genre-sizing notes from tops-by-tag plus period comparison in the web UI, exports CSVs, and aggregates by tag and country in spreadsheets. He doubts single-app retention estimates (the G2 complaint) so he cross-checks against context before quoting them.
- **Weekly ritual:** Genre heat check: aggregate downloads/revenue for a handful of tags (merge, match-3, 4X) across key countries, plus which publishers are gaining chart presence.
- **Frustration:** Summing estimates across a tag's top apps by hand, every week, because no endpoint aggregates across tags; and not knowing whether a single app's retention number is normal for its genre.

## Candidates (pre-cut)

1. **Chart diff** — `appmagic chart-diff --sort grossing --store 2 --country US` (defaults to latest two local snapshots; `--from/--to` for explicit dates). One-liner: entered/dropped/moved report between two synced top-chart snapshots. Persona: Deniz. Source: (b) (also Product Thesis names "chart-snapshot diffing"). Kill/keep: passes all checks; pure local SQLite, mechanical, verifiable with synthetic snapshots. KEEP.
   Long: Use this command to see rank movement, new entrants, and dropouts between two synced top-chart snapshots for any chart sort. Do NOT use this command to track newly soft-launched titles across test markets; use 'soft-launch-radar' instead. Do NOT use it for market-size aggregates by tag; use 'tag-rollup' instead.

2. **Soft-launch radar** — `appmagic soft-launch-radar --countries PH,CA,AU --since 30d [--publisher <name>] [--tag <tag>]`. One-liner: first-seen detection of new soft-launch titles per test market from synced soft-launches chart snapshots. Persona: Deniz. Source: (a)+(b) (thesis names "soft-launch watchlists"; spec has a dedicated tops soft-launches operation). Kill/keep: passes; local snapshots, mechanical first-seen computation. KEEP.
   Long: Use this command to find newly detected soft-launch titles with first-seen dates per test market, optionally filtered by publisher or tag. Do NOT use it for general top-chart rank movement; use 'chart-diff' instead.

3. **Watchlist + weekly report** — `appmagic watchlist add "Royal Match"` / `appmagic watchlist report --country US --metrics downloads,revenue,retention --since 7d`. One-liner: persistent local competitor set; one command pulls all metrics for the whole set via the batched charts `requests[]` envelope and prints a side-by-side table. Persona: Mara. Source: (a). Kill/keep: scope-creep check triggered ("monitor"), descoped to add/list/report subcommands in one file, no background process. Verifiability flagged: live dogfood skipped this run (no credentials); structure verifiable against recorded fixtures. KEEP.
   Long (on report): Use this command to pull a side-by-side weekly metrics table for your saved competitor set. Do NOT use it to benchmark one app against its genre cohort median; use 'retention-benchmark' instead.

4. **ASO movers** — `appmagic aso-movers <app> --country US --store 2 --since 7d [--dataset asa]`. One-liner: gained/lost/moved keyword ranks between two dates computed from the aso series endpoints. Persona: Priya. Source: (a). Kill/keep: passes; entitlement-gated group, but read-only on the same Basic auth, honest 403 UX per Build Priority 5. KEEP. Long: none.

5. **Entitlements probe** — `appmagic entitlements [--refresh]`. One-liner: issues one cheap canonical request per endpoint group (20 spec tags), records 200/401/403/429 per group into SQLite, and reports exactly which groups YOUR contract includes; doctor and per-command 403 messages read this cache. Persona: all four; Priya most acutely. Source: (b)+(e)+(f) (per-contract gating pattern; User Vision "anyone with paid access, anywhere"; G2 complaint 3 "features gated to higher tiers without upfront transparency"; unauth probe sweep observed 403s on steam/* and period-comparison/*). Kill/keep: passes; composes with (does not re-propose) absorbed row 23's doctor summary, this is the engine that populates it with per-group detail. KEEP. Long: none.

6. **Resolve pipeline** — `appmagic resolve <name|store-id|url>`. One-liner: one-shot resolution to united ID + per-store IDs. Persona: all. Source: (b). Kill/keep: CUT NOW, already absorbed: Table Stakes "search → resolve → fetch pipelines" plus absorbed rows 1-2 (united-ID local cache). Re-proposal, not novel.

7. **Tag rollup (market sizing)** — `appmagic tag-rollup --tags merge-2,match-3 --country JP --metric revenue --period 30d`. One-liner: resolves tag IDs from the synced taxonomy, pulls top apps per tag, then one batched charts call to sum downloads/revenue per tag, side by side. Persona: Tomas. Source: (b)+(c) (Top Workflow 3; thesis names "market rollups"). Kill/keep: reimplementation check examined: the API has no multi-tag aggregation endpoint, so local summation is legitimate synthesis, not a fake API call. Feasibility costs one extra composition step (tops figures are not guaranteed per entry, so estimates come from the batched charts call). KEEP.
   Long: Use this command for aggregate market sizing (summed downloads or revenue) across one or more tags. Do NOT use it to list which apps moved within a chart; use 'chart-diff' instead.

8. **Similarity graph walk** — `appmagic similar-walk <app> --depth 2`. One-liner: BFS over applications similarity/competitors edges, deduped and ranked by edge overlap, to map a competitor neighborhood. Persona: Mara (when scoping). Source: (b) (thesis names it). Kill/keep: passes mechanical checks but weekly-use is doubtful; defer to Pass 3.

9. **Quota budget** — `appmagic quota`. One-liner: persists X-RateLimit-* headers from every response and projects calls remaining today. Persona: Tomas. Source: (b) (thesis "rate-limit budget awareness"). Kill/keep: overlap risk with absorbed row 24 (rate-limit visibility + adaptive 429 limiter already committed); defer to Pass 3.

10. **Freshness report** — `appmagic freshness [--range 2026-06-01:2026-06-09]`. One-liner: aggregates all three last-date watermarks (core, steam, period-comparison) plus local sync ages and warns when a requested range exceeds the watermark. Persona: Tomas. Source: (f) (G2 complaint 2: recent-date lag). Kill/keep: overlap risk with absorbed row 19/23 (last-date already a doctor liveness check); defer to Pass 3.

11. **Publisher momentum** — `appmagic publisher-momentum --country US --sort grossing --weeks 4`. One-liner: group synced chart snapshots by publisher to rank week-over-week chart-presence gainers. Persona: Tomas. Source: (c) (Top Workflow 4). Kill/keep: framework `analytics --type top_snapshots --group-by publisher` covers most of this natively; defer to Pass 3.

12. **Retention benchmark** — `appmagic retention-benchmark "Royal Match" --tag match-3 --country US --top 20`. One-liner: pulls the target's retention curve plus its tag cohort's curves in one batched `requests[]` envelope and prints app-vs-cohort-median (D1/D7/D30) so a doubted estimate gets context. Persona: Tomas, Mara. Source: (c)+(f) (G2 complaint 1: retention accuracy doubts; brief's Community Signal explicitly maps this to "cross-source sanity surface"). Kill/keep: passes; mechanical median, no LLM; live-gated for dogfood this run, flagged. KEEP.
   Long: Use this command to compare one app's retention curve against the median of its tag cohort. Do NOT use it for multi-metric comparisons across your saved competitor set; use 'watchlist report' instead.

13. **Live-ops overlay** — `appmagic liveops-overlay "Royal Match" --country WW --since 90d`. One-liner: joins live-ops events for an app with its revenue/downloads series by date window, emitting an event-vs-metric-delta table ("did the event spike revenue"). Persona: Mara, Deniz. Source: (c) (live-ops is "unique in category at API level" per absorbed row 12; the join is new). Kill/keep: passes; date-window join is mechanical. KEEP.
   Long: Use this command to correlate a single app's live-ops events with its revenue or download movement. Do NOT use it for plain cross-app metric comparison; use 'watchlist report' instead.

14. **Steam crossover** — `appmagic steam-crossover <publisher>`. One-liner: find a mobile publisher's Steam presence and compare metrics. Persona: Tomas. Source: (b). Kill/keep: steam/* group 403'd even in the anonymous sweep (entitlement-gated for many contracts) and the workflow is episodic; defer to Pass 3.

15. **SDK change watch** — `appmagic sdk-watch --since 30d`. One-liner: surface watchlist apps that changed SDKs via sdkint changed. Persona: Mara. Source: (b). Kill/keep: thin wrapper over one endpoint plus a watchlist filter; cadence is monthly; defer to Pass 3.

16. **ASA movers** — `appmagic asa-movers <app> --country US --since 7d`. One-liner: same delta computation as ASO movers but over Apple Search Ads terms. Persona: Priya. Source: (a). Kill/keep: duplicate mechanics of candidate 4 behind a rarer entitlement (asa group); merge into `aso-movers --dataset asa`; defer formal kill to Pass 3.

## Survivors and kills

**1. chart-diff** — Weekly use: yes, it IS Deniz's Monday movers report. Wrapper vs leverage: no single endpoint diffs snapshots; pure local leverage. Transcendence: local SQLite snapshot history (calls `hintIfUnsynced/hintIfStale` on the snapshots resource; `// pp:data-source local`). Sibling kill: publisher-momentum (its group-by view is covered by framework `analytics --type top_snapshots --group-by publisher` plus this command). Buildability: hand-code. Long validity: references soft-launch-radar and tag-rollup, both survive. Score: Domain 3, Pain 2, Feasibility 2, Research 2 (Product Thesis + Top Workflow 2) = 9/10.

**2. soft-launch-radar** — Weekly use: yes, soft-launch scouting is the other half of the Monday ritual. Wrapper: the soft-launches endpoint returns today's list; first-seen-per-market requires history it does not keep. Transcendence: local SQLite first-seen index across test-market snapshots (`// pp:data-source local`, sync hints). Sibling kill: steam-crossover (episodic, entitlement-gated scouting; the weekly new-titles scan lives here). Buildability: hand-code. Long validity: references chart-diff, survives. Score: Domain 3, Pain 2, Feasibility 2, Research 2 (thesis "soft-launch watchlists" + dedicated spec operation) = 9/10.

**3. watchlist + watchlist report** — Weekly use: yes, Mara's core ritual. Wrapper: no; one command replaces N per-app chart pulls plus a hand-built spreadsheet via the batched `requests[]` envelope. Transcendence: local watchlist table + service-specific batch envelope + agent-shaped comparison table (`// pp:data-source live` on report, local subcommands for add/list). Sibling kill: similar-walk (episodic set-discovery; the watchlist consumes IDs however found). Buildability: hand-code. Long validity: references retention-benchmark, survives. Score: Domain 3, Pain 2, Feasibility 1 (new watchlist table), Research 2 (Top Workflow 1 + batch envelope in spec) = 8/10.

**4. entitlements** — Weekly use: probe itself is occasional, but the cached result is consulted by doctor, agents, and every 403 message session-by-session; kept despite the soft flag because Pain is explicit. Wrapper: no endpoint reports entitlements; it is synthesized from systematic probing. Transcendence: service-specific content pattern (per-contract group gating) + local SQLite cache + agent-shaped output (`// pp:data-source auto`). Sibling kill: quota (its delta over absorbed row 24's rate-limit visibility was too thin to justify a command). Buildability: hand-code. Long: none. Score: Domain 2, Pain 3 (explicit G2 complaint), Feasibility 2, Research 2 (G2/OMR + unauth-probe-sweep artifact) = 9/10.

**5. tag-rollup** — Weekly use: yes, Tomas's genre heat check. Wrapper: no; the API has no cross-tag aggregation endpoint. Transcendence: cross-entity join (synced taxonomy + tops + batched charts) with local summation (`// pp:data-source auto`, sync hints on taxonomy). Sibling kill: publisher-momentum (the other snapshot-rollup candidate; framework analytics covers it). Buildability: hand-code. Long validity: references chart-diff, survives. Score: Domain 3, Pain 2, Feasibility 1 (figures require the extra batched charts step), Research 2 (Top Workflow 3 + thesis "market rollups") = 8/10.

**6. retention-benchmark** — Weekly use: yes; any week a retention number goes in a deck it needs cohort context. Wrapper: no; cohort selection + batched pulls + median is synthesis. Transcendence: batch envelope + tag taxonomy + computed cohort median, agent-shaped (`// pp:data-source live`). Sibling kill: freshness (the other complaint-born trust feature; its remaining delta beyond absorbed row 19/23's doctor watermark was too thin). Buildability: hand-code. Long validity: references watchlist report, survives. Score: Domain 3, Pain 3 (explicit G2 complaint, brief maps it to a command), Feasibility 2, Research 2 = 10/10.

**7. aso-movers** — Weekly use: yes, Priya's weekly client report. Wrapper: borderline (aso series is one endpoint) but the gained/lost/moved delta set across two dates is computed, not returned, by the API. Transcendence: service-specific delta synthesis with agent-shaped output (`// pp:data-source live`). Sibling kill: asa-movers (identical mechanics behind a rarer entitlement; merged as `--dataset asa`). Buildability: hand-code. Long: none. Score: Domain 3, Pain 2, Feasibility 2, Research 1 (Top Workflow 5 only) = 8/10.

**8. liveops-overlay** — Weekly use: yes for Mara's competitor deck (live-ops is AppMagic's flagship differentiator). Wrapper: no; it joins two endpoint families by date window. Transcendence: cross-endpoint date join + computed deltas (`// pp:data-source live`). Sibling kill: sdk-watch (the other "what did the competitor change" candidate; monthly cadence, thin wrapper). Buildability: hand-code. Long validity: references watchlist report, survives. Score: Domain 3, Pain 2, Feasibility 2, Research 1 (live-ops uniqueness per absorb analysis) = 8/10.
